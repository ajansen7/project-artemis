# Blog Archive Analysis
Last updated: 2026-03-21
Source: https://thetechnicalpmlab.substack.com/ (Substack)
Total posts imported: 4

## Recurring Themes
- **Agentic AI and practical orchestration** — most posts touch on building with AI agents, whether Project Artemis or side projects. This is the dominant thread.
- **The "builder mindset" for PMs** — the throughline across all four posts is that technical PMs should be hands-on builders, not just managers of builders.
- **AI evaluation and quality** — one dedicated post on evals, but the theme surfaces elsewhere (grounding AI output, preventing hallucination creep, knowing what AI can do natively).
- **Velocity and leverage from modern tooling** — recurring celebration of how dramatically the cost of building has dropped (one-hour portfolio, one-evening game, 20-minute spec).
- **"Activation over embellishment"** — the philosophy that AI should surface authentic experience, not manufacture a persona. Appears explicitly in the Artemis post but is latent throughout.
- **Learning by doing** — every post anchors insights in a specific hands-on experience rather than abstract theory.

## Voice Patterns
- **First person, experience-first** — posts open by planting the reader in a real situation Alex faced, not a broad industry claim. "I recently hit a wall..." / "When our team started shipping..."
- **Conversational but substantive** — sentences are direct and move fast. No academic hedging. Comfortable using "I" without apology.
- **Section headers carry weight** — headers are punchy and conceptual ("The Opus Trap," "Reasoning is the Bottleneck, Not Execution") rather than generic ("Background," "Results").
- **Closing with a challenge** — nearly every post ends with a direct call-to-action aimed at the reader ("Let's build something," "drop a link in the comments," "maybe it's time to build it").
- **Practical takeaways labeled clearly** — "Key Insight:" appears as a named section, signaling Alex values giving readers a crisp, quotable takeaway.
- **Analogies and naming** — coins phrases like "the Opus Trap," "lossy compression," "chattery agent," "activation over embellishment." Strong tendency to name things.

## Post Formats That Appear Most
- **Personal narrative + lesson** — lead with a real experience, extract a transferable principle (all 4 posts use this)
- **Numbered mistakes / lessons** ("Mistake #1," "Mistake #2") — used in the evals post for clarity when walking through a list of failures
- **Case study within a narrative** — the "building for fun" post nests two mini case studies inside a broader argument
- **Architecture walkthrough** — the Artemis post goes deep on the system design, component by component
